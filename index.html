Aberrations

Aberrations are mutated variants of fish in DREDGE that are more difficult to find than their "normal" counterparts. They are described by the Fishmonger as 'corrupted', by the in-game text as 'grotesque' and is the first sign in the game that something is not right. The purple text box used in descriptions of Aberrated fish (same background color of inventory tiles for Aberrations, similar to Trophy fish) is described in the options as 'Important Color'.

The pursuit Grotesque Fish has you bring an Aberration (no matter which) to the Fishmonger. He slits it open and finds the handkerchief, which he gives to you (this unlocks the Collector's mission). There is another quest where you give another Aberrated fish to the Fishmonger, and it is implied he eats it. Afterwards, he doesn't feel well, but he seems to be fine as usual in later days. Aberrated fish are worth significantly more than their normal counterparts.

Judging by the Encyclopedia, nearly half of the 128 fish that will be found in the game are Aberrations of other, normal fish. There are 13 aberrations found in the demo, 9 of which are known: 3 for the Blue Mackerel (Grotesque Mackerel, Lumpy Mackerel and Many-Eyed Mackerel), 3 for the Cod (All-Seeing Cod, Fanged Cod and Three-Headed Cod), 2 for the Grey Eel (Barbed Eel, Host Eel), 2 for the Gulf Flounder (Cyclopean Flounder, Riddled Flounder), 2 for the Arrow Squid (Snag Squid, Brood Squid), 2 for the Black Grouper (Tusked Grouper, Voltaic Grouper), and 1 for the Stingray (Shard Ray). There are no Aberrated versions of the two crabs.

Aberrated Fish can be found in patches of disturbed water only if the player makes no mistakes while reeling in the fish. Furthermore, Aberrated fish are found in greater frequency in patches of disturbed water with embers and a blue distortion above them still noting that the player makes no mistakes while catching the fish (right/above).

Aberrated fish are part of the eldritch horror aspect of DREDGE, and it is implied that they are incubators of some sort (the Cyclopean Flounder having an egg in its head, the Brood Squid and Host Eel's obvious implications, etc.) Some aberrated fish also remind of radioactively mutated animals (e.g., the Lumpy Mackerel and the Tusked Grouper), and some are simply strange-looking (e.g., the All-Seeing Cod, the Snag Squid and the Shard Ray).

Catching Strategies

Trying to catch Grotesque Fish from non-glowing, disturbed water can be irritating, due to the low chance of success and the requirement to perform the catching mini-game perfectly. However, if you leave the fishing minigame after make a mistake, the mistake is removed, allowing you another opportunity at catching an aberrated fish.

This is especially useful while trying to fill the encyclopedia, as certain fish spawn in few locations with only 1-2 fish per disturbed water patch (e.g. Devil Rays, Hammerhead Sharks, etc.).
